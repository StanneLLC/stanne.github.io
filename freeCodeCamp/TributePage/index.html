Tribute to Mike Royko

aka Slats Grobnick

Photo © CHICAGO TRIBUNE/GETTY

If you needed to find someone who could be funny, and no nonsense at the same time, you need look no further than Mike Royko. A man not afraid to tell it like it is, and yet somehow amuse you at the same time. A journalist among journalists, and gone before his time. I have yet to find a columnist I've enjoyed reading as much as him.

  • 1932 - Born September 19 Chicago, Illinois
  • 1952 - Enlisted in the U.S. Air Force
  • 1955 - Began his newsman's career as a columnist for The O'Hare News (Air Force base newspaper)
  • 1964 - Success earns him a daily column in the Daily News
  • 1964 - Slats Grobnik is born. Referred to as Mike's "alter ego".
  • 1972 - Receives a Pulitzer Prize for commentary as a Daily News columnist.
  • 1983 - Inducted as a Laureate of The Lincoln Academy of Illinois
  • 1983 - Awarded the Order of Lincoln ( the State's highest honor)
  • 1990 - Wins the National Press Club Lifetime Achievement Award
  • 1995 - Wins the Damon Runyon Award
  • 1997 - Dies of a brain aneurysm at age 64
  • 2011 - Inducted into the Chicago Literary Hall of Fame
He was also a published author. His works include:
  1. 1967 - Up Against It.
  2. 1968 - I May Be Wrong, But I Doubt It.
  3. 1971 - Boss: Richard J. Daley of Chicago
  4. 1973 - Slats Grobnik and Some Other Friends.
  5. 1983 - Sez Who? Sez Me
  6. 1985 - Like I Was Sayin
  7. 1989 - Dr. Kookie, You're Right
After his death, the following were published:
  1. 2000 - One More Time: The Best of Mike Royko
  2. 2001 - For the Love of Mike: More of the Best of Mike Royko
  3. 2010 - Early Royko: Up Against It in Chicago
  4. 2010 - Royko in Love: Mike's Letters to Carol
You can read more about him on his Wikipedia page.
